TJ Holmes reveals he's 'interested in getting married again' after being 'turned off' to tying the k

TJ Holmes has revealed he wants to get married again after divorcing his second wife, Marilee Fiebig.

The ex-GMA3 co-host finalized his divorce from Marilee, 46, in October 2023 after splitting over his supposed affair with Amy Robach, 50.

TJ, 46, opened up about his hopes to remarry on Tuesday's installment of his and Amy's new podcast, Amy & TJ.

Throughout the episode, the couple shared what they learned in 2023 after their relationship was made public.

A note on TJ's list shocked listeners as he admitted he hopes to tie the knot for a third time.

"I learned that I'm actually interested in getting married again," the TV star confessed.

"Look, nobody wants to be divorced once, and certainly nobody wants to be divorced twice, and it didn't turn me off to relationships; it turned me off to the idea of getting married again. But, here I am," he added.

TJ split from his first wife, journalist Amy Ferson, in 2007.

He later exchanged vows with Marilee - the mother of his 10-year-old daughter, Sabine - in 2010 before they parted ways late last year.

TJ had already teased that he and his girlfriend and former GMA3 co-host, Amy, secretly got married.

ALREADY MARRIED?

On an earlier episode of their podcast, Amy and TJ answered fans' questions about their whirlwind romance, with one asking if they'd ever consider getting matching tattoos.

"How do you know we haven't already? We'll leave it at that," TJ replied.

"Getting a tattoo together. That's more of a commitment than marriage."

TJ then joked that he and Amy already made a major commitment.

"Should we just tell everybody? We've got tattoos, we're married, and we live together," he teased.

Amy laughed at TJ's comment but didn't clarify further.

TROUBLE IN PARADISE

However, the pair have admitted they've faced struggles in their relationship.

On last week's podcast episode, Amy and TJ revealed they recently endured a big fight that drove them into couple's therapy.
