The 17 Best Anti-Aging Products of 2024

Fine lines, wrinkles, crow’s feet, sun spots, lackluster skin — these are all common skincare concerns that creep up as we get older, whether it be due to the natural effects of aging or spending too much time in the sun without SPF. Anti-aging products exist to support the overall health of the skin as well as address some of these woes, especially when powered by potent anti-aging ingredients such as ceramides, retinol, vitamin C, and hyaluronic acid. 

“There are many benefits to using an anti-aging skincare regimen, including a reduction of fine lines and wrinkles,” Georgia Smith, a cosmetic chemist and founder of Skin Sister, confirms. She notes that anti-aging products can be used as a preventative measure and to address pre-existing concerns in mature skin. “Usually anywhere from 25-plus years old is a good time to start using an anti-aging skincare regimen to slow down the process [of aging] and make skin appear more youthful.” 

Whether you’re taking preventative measures or looking for that one skincare product that will bring your youthful glow back to the complexion’s surface, there is a range of anti-aging products that can help you yield the results you want and support your skin’s health along the way. We scoured the internet, tested products, and asked top skin experts about what to look for in anti-aging products to determine the best options. 

From skincare staples like face oil and retinol cream to makeup formulas like CC cream and setting spray, here are the best anti-aging products of 2024.

How to Pick the Right Anti-Aging Products

Ingredients 

“Look for anti-aging products that contain active ingredients that have been shown to be effective in improving the appearance of aging skin,” says Dr. Viseslav Tonkovic-Capin, MD, a double board-certified dermatologist and editor at DermBoard. He recommends using anti-aging products that contain such ingredients as retinol, vitamin C, hyaluronic acid, glycerin, and alpha- and beta-hydroxy acids.

Sunscreen 

If you don’t already have a good, high-quality sunscreen (like the Supergoop! Daily Dose Vitamin C + SPF 40 Serum), Dr. Tonkovic-Capin says to invest in one and also look for products like daytime moisturizer and makeup that incorporate SPF in their formulas. “Sun exposure is the most detrimental cause of skin aging, so it’s important to choose products that contain sun-protective ingredients,” he explains. In addition to SPF, sun protective ingredients also include antioxidants like vitamin C, which can protect the complexion from environmental aggressors that impact aging, including the sun’s harmful UV rays and pollution. 

Skin Type 

“Consider your skin type when shopping for anti-aging products as some products may be too harsh for sensitive skin, while others may not provide enough moisture for dry skin,” says Dr. Tonkovic-Capin. To make things easier, he recommends choosing products that are specifically formulated for your skin type, such as a facial moisturizer for oily skin, to avoid irritation or other issues.

If you have sensitive skin, Dr. Sandy Skotnicki, a board-certified dermatologist and author of Beyond Soap, says be weary of retinol as it can be irritating to the complexion. She also recommends products that are fragrance-free (especially if using potent ingredients like retinol) since “fragrance can often add to irritation.” 

Price 

With high-quality and potent ingredients involved, your skincare bill can add up quickly, so it’s important to also look at the price and where you can invest. “Anti-aging products can range from a few to hundreds of dollars per bottle,” says Dr. Tonkovic-Capin. He adds that, while price doesn’t always indicate effectiveness, it’s important to consider your budget and how much you’re willing to spend — after all, you’ll eventually need to restock. 

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do any creams actually get rid of wrinkles?

    Not all anti-aging creams are created equal, which means not all of them will target the look of wrinkles. According to Dr. Tonkovic-Capin, some creams, especially formulas with retinol (like the Paula’s Choice Intensive Repair Cream) can eliminate fine wrinkles over several months of use. “Still, no cream will eliminate all wrinkles,” Dr. Tonkovic-Capin adds. 

  • When should I start using anti-aging products?

    “The introduction of cosmeceuticals that target aging can be started at any age, but generally we recommend them around 25 to 30 years of age,” says Dr. Skotnicki. However, some circumstances — such as where you live — can influence when to start using anti-aging products. “If you live in a major city where pollution is high or live near the equator where the UV index is higher, starting an anti-aging product should occur even earlier due to the increased oxidative stress associated with pollution and UV exposure, which are the main drivers of skin aging,” Dr. Skotnicki adds.

  • Are there any side effects from using retinol?

    When new to retinol, Smith says that most people will experience some slight skin irritation and even breakouts; however, this usually subsides after a month of use. “Retinol binds to receptors in the skin and this leads to activation of skin turnover [which] can lead to dryness and skin irritation,” Dr. Skotnicki further explains. Retinol can also make your skin more sensitive to the sun and is therefore recommended that you use it at night and apply sunscreen during the day.

  • Is it possible to use too much anti-aging product?

    “You can’t overdo anti-aging lotions and creams, but it is possible to use too much retinol,” says Smith. For over-the-counter retinol formulas, she recommends starting gradually one to two times a week, then increasing as your skin adapts. “If you use a retinol-based product as well as a chemical exfoliation product together, you are also over-doing it and most likely stripping your skin’s natural barrier, creating skin issues like irritation.”
