What happened to Dog the Bounty Hunter star Youngblood and where is he now?

TIM "Youngblood" Chapman was known as Dog the Bounty Hunter's left-hand man in the explosive series that ended in 2012.

Since then, fans are wondering what happened to the star and where he is now.

Who is Youngblood?

Born May 13, 1965, Tim "Youngblood" Chapman, 56, is known as a retired American bounty hunter.

From 2004 to 2012, he was known for assisting Duane Chapman, who famously goes by the name Dog the Bounty Hunter, on his show catching fugitives.

Although Youngblood and Duane share the same last name, the two are not related.

Outside of television, not much is known about Youngblood as he had managed to stay out of the media following the ending of the show.

However, it is known that he is a father to three children, including Tim Chapman Jr, Storm Hunter Chapman and Thunder Cloud Chapman.

Where is he now?

Since Dog the Bounty Hunter ended, little is known about Youngblood's whereabouts.

The California native is a single father and is said to have retired from bounty hunting altogether.

He divorced his wife Davina Chapman in 2009 for allegedly cheating on him and has not been in a public relationship since.

After years of legal trouble and a fast-paced life hunting criminals, it's plausible that Youngblood has found respite away from the spotlight.

Was Youngblood arrested?

In 2008, Youngblood was arrested and charged with indecent exposure in Honolulu, Hawaii.

At the time, a woman claimed that she saw him masturbating inside his parked truck.

At the trial, Youngblood explained that he had spilled juice all over himself and was using baby wipes to clean it up.

He was later found not guilty of all charges and free to go.

Following the verdict, he told reporters that the week-long trial was a "waste of taxpayer money," according to Hawaii News Now.

As of 2021, that is the only trouble he has had with the law, unlike his former partner, who once went to jail after being convicted of first-degree murder, and owes over $1million in unpaid legal fees.
