Career

At all levels, James Forrest excelled for South Ayr Boys Club. He was discovered when he was 11 years old by Celtic’s Ayrshire talent scout, Jim Began. Kilmarnock and Ayr United were also interested in him in addition to Rangers. His coach, Martin Millar, believes that joining Celtic’s Hamilton coaching school when he was 13 years old and signing a youth contract with them was crucial to his development.

On August 30, 2009, he agreed to a four-year contract with Celtic. On May 1 of the following season, against Motherwell, he made his first-team Celtic debut, coming on as a late substitute and scoring the club’s third goal in a 4-0 triumph. He received the Scottish Youth Cup in 2009–10 after beating Rangers 2-0 at Hampden Park.

In a July 2010 Champions League qualification game against Braga, he made his debut for Celtic in Europe. He also won the Man of the Encounter trophy a few days later for his performance in the Emirates Cup match against Olympique Lyonnais. In 2012, he participated in all four of Celtic’s Champions League qualifiers, assisting them to reach the group stage for the first time since 2008.

In a Champions League qualification game against Cliftonville in July 2013, he scored his first European goal for Celtic. He and Celtic reached a three-year contract agreement in August 2016. On October 30, 2019, he was awarded a new deal that will keep him with the team until 2023.

International Career

James Forrest played for Scotland at every youth level, gaining 13 caps for the under-19 team, where he scored two goals, and four more for the under-21 squad. He participated in his debut senior international game on May 29, 2011, against the Republic of Ireland.

He participated in the UEFA Euro 2020 Scotland team. On June 14, 2021, at Hampden Park, he made his sole appearance in the competition as a substitute in Scotland’s 2-0 defeat to the Czech Republic.
