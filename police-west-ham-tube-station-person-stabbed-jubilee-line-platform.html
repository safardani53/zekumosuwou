Police swarm West Ham tube station after person stabbed on Jubilee Line platform

COPS swarmed West Ham tube station after a teenage boy was stabbed multiple times near the platform today.

British Transport Police said that a second male was later found with knife wounds at Abbey Road DLR Station after the pair "had an altercation in the stairwell".

TFL said that West Ham is part closed, and that Jubilee Line trains aren't stopping at the station "due to a customer incident".

Met Police told Sun Online that cops rushed to West Ham Underground Station at 18.51pm on Saturday to reports of a stabbing.

A spokesperson confirmed that a teenage male was found with stab injuries.

He was treated at the scene before being taken to hospital.

"We await an update on his condition", they added.

A short time later, "another male was found with stab injuries at Abbey Road DLR Station.

"He was also treated by paramedics before being taken to hospital.

"There have been no arrests. A crime scene is in place.

"The investigation will be led by British Transport Police," the Met added.

STAIRWELL FIGHT

A spokesperson for BTP told Sun Online: "It appears that two men met as they passed each other on a stairway at West Ham station.

"Following what seems to have been an altercation between the two men, one male received stab wounds and collapsed at the station.

"The other male, who also received stab wounds, got onto a train to Abbey Road DLR Station."

While the ages of neither man have been released, both are understood to have received multiple stab wounds.

Their condition is not known, said the spokesperson.

Passengers told on social media how they were ushered back onto trains before 7pm.

One man tweeted: “Someone just been stabbed at West Ham platform, station closed. Avoid [the] station."

Another warned on Twitter: “Big police incident at West Ham tube.

"Trains not stopping, just went through and saw police with somebody handcuffed on the platform, and also vans outside the station."

A fellow passer-by also tweeted: “Major incidents all roads & trains closed. Avoid West Ham station.”

One woman thanked paramedics and cops "for such a quick response and keeping London safe after another stabbing".

Another passenger tweeted about a "big police incident at West Ham tube - trains not stopping.

"Just went through and saw police... and also vans outside the station."

Police are asking anyone who was in or around West Ham Underground Station at about 6.50pm this evening, or at Abbey Road DLR Station shortly afterwards and "saw anything suspicious, to please get in touch".
