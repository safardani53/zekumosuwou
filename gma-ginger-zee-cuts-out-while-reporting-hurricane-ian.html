GMAs Ginger Zee cuts out while reporting from tough conditions in Hurricane Ian

GOOD Morning America meteorologist Ginger Zee has been cut off from the broadcast while reporting on Hurricane Ian in Florida.

She stood in front of wreckage from a hotel and other "tough conditions."

On Thursday's show, Michael Strahan, George Stephanopoulos, and Amy Robach sat behind the desk.

They got right into the breaking news of Hurricane Ian taking over Florida.

After giving some background information, they passed the segment over to Chief Meteorologist Ginger Zee, who showed b-roll of the damage the storm has caused so far as she did a voiceover to let viewers know what was going on.

She stood in front of wreckage from a hotel, whose entire back was ripped off.

After the video footage was done, the camera came back to Ginger, who was standing on a balcony of a hotel with winds and rain pouring in behind her.

They then showed more footage and went back to the GMA host, who was in a calmer place now.

However, she only got to talk for about a minute before the feed cut out and she froze on screen.

It then went back to George, who said seriously: "Ah. The conditions are tough right there. We saw Ginger drop out, but we saw the scale of the storm."

'STAY SAFE'

Ginger has been at the front of this storm since before it started.

Fans have been worried for her as she updates them on social media.

On Wednesday afternoon, the 41-year-old shocked fans when she shared a video from the eye of the storm.

The weatherwoman sported a blue rainjacket and ABC News hat while drenched in water from the category 4 hurricane.

"Hello and good afternoon, we are in the eyewall," she announced.

The TV personality then filmed outside of the balcony of her hotel as wild winds and rain battered the Florida coastline, flattening palm trees to the ground.

The beach and pool area disappeared underneath inches and inches of water as Ginger kept fans updated on the status of the storm.

But fans were terrified for her safety as they raced to the comments to share their concern.

"Wooza!! Stay safe! Appreciate the updates lots of friends in Florida!!" one commented.

"Holy s**t balls!!!!’ Stay safe and am loving watching nature through your reports," a second agreed.

A third remarked: "Stay safe girl!" while a fourth gushed: "Oh my god ginger!! Please be safe! & Ian spare my beautiful Cape Coral please."

"I pray that you and your team stay safe!" a fifth wished.

DANGEROUS CONDITIONS

Earlier this week, the weatherwoman was nearly blown over by the oncoming Hurricane Ian as she reported live from the storm.

Good Morning America took to Twitter to post a snippet from her segment as she stood drenched in the wild rain wearing a dark blue jacket.

Trees behind Ginger whipped in the wind as she struggled to stand upright during her report.

Fans rushed to reply as they expressed their concern for Ginger as she braced for the category 4 hurricane.

"We do not need visuals of people getting rained on. You’re putting your employees live’s at risk. Tell them all to go inside," one ranted.

"Why do you have your reporters outside???" a second asked.

A third complained: "Never understood the logic of this. We know it's windy and raining."
