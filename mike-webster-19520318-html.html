Michael Lewis Webster is an American professional soccer player who served as the centerpiece of the National Football Federation from 1974 to 1990 alongside Pittsburgh Steelers and Kansas City Chiefs. He was a member of the 1997 Pro Football Hall of Fame, Class. Nicknamed "Iron Mike", Webster fixed the Steelers' attack line for most of their Super Bowl four-win streak from 1974 to 1979. and is considered by many to be the largest center in NFL history. Webster was the first former NFL player to be diagnosed with a chronic traumatic encephalopathy. Since his death, he has become a symbol of the NFL's head wounds and the constant debate over the player's safety. His doctors believed that many times the concussions during his work damaged his frontal lobe, causing cognitive dysfunction. Webster died in 2002 at the age of 50 from a heart attack.
