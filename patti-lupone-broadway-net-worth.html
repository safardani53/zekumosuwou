Who is Patti LuPone? | The US Sun

SINCE 1972, Patti LuPone has appeared in several Broadway productions.

Patti is currently starring in Company on Broadway, a Stephen Sondheim-composed musical comedy.

Who is Patti LuPone?

Born on April 21, 1949, Patti LuPone is a native of Long Island, New York.

Her parents both worked in education - her mother was a librarian at the local university, and her father was an English teacher at Walt Whitman High School.

Patti and her fellow classmates became the first graduating class of Juilliard's Drama Division in 1972.

Her brother, Robert LuPone, is also a Tony-nominated actor.

Post-graduation, Patti joined The Acting Company, formed by John Houseman.

Her Broadway debut came in 1973 with the role of Irina in The Three Sisters.

What awards has Patti LuPone won?

Patti LuPone has earned several coveted awards throughout her lengthy career, both on-stage and on-screen.

Out of eight Tony nominations, Patti has won two for Best Actress in a Musical - one for Evita in 1980 and another for Gypsy in 2008.

She is also the owner of two Grammy Awards; both received in 2009 for her work on Weill: Rise and Fall of the City of Mahagonny.

Patti earned two Emmy nominations in the 1990s for her roles on various television shows.

Should she earn an Emmy, Patti will only be an Oscar away from being named an EGOT.

Is Patti LuPone married?

Patti LuPone married Matthew Johnston on stage at the Vivian Beaumont Theatre in December of 1988.

Together, the two share one child: Joshua Luke Johnston.
