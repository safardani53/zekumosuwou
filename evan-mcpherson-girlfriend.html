Short Bio On Evan McPherson

Evan McPherson is a professional football player playing for the Cincinnati Bengals as a kicker.

McPherson was born on July 21, 1999, to Ladon McPherson and  Amber McPherson in Fort Payne, Alabama, USA. He has two siblings, Logan and Alex.

McPherson grew up in Fort Payne and went to Fort Payne City School. His older brother, Logan, used to play football, inspiring him to try his luck in the game.

After graduating high school, McPherson went to the University of Florida for college studies and played football for the college team. 

The Cincinnati Bengals select Evan in the 5th round, overall 141st of the 2021 NFL draft. Because of his place-picking skills and techniques, the officials named Evan the starter for the 2021 regular season.

In 2021, Evan earned his first AFC Special Teams Player of The Week after scoring a 33-yard game-winning goal leading the Bengals to victory.

McPherson established a franchise record for the longest field goal with a distance of 59 yards in the 2022 Bengals’ season-opening contest versus the Pittsburgh Steelers.
