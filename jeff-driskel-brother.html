Jeff Driskel Brother | Early Life, Family & Education

Jason Driskel, the younger brother of Jeff, was born to his parents, Jerry and Mary Driskel, on November 12, 1995, in Oviedo, Florida, United States.

Both his parents served in the U.S. military. His mother, Mary, served in the U.S. coast guard for many years. 

Jerry had joined the navy when he was just seventeen. He traveled to different places worldwide, serving his country, and eventually retired after twenty years of service.

Being the children of military personnel, Jason and his brother grew up in a disciplined environment. They spent about three years of their childhood in Sasebo when Jerry was posted in Japan.

Jason began playing baseball and football at a young age. His elder brother, Jeff, was a comparatively better baseball player than him, but he was not that far behind.

While in Japan, their parents had admitted them to a Little League team, where they became local superstars because of their performances, as the other teammates lacked as much athleticism as the Driskel brothers had.

After returning to the U.S., Jason attended Hagerty High School in Oviedo, Florida, where he played football. Aside from being good at sports, Jason was academically gifted and always brought excellent grades.

After graduation, he enrolled in the Florida Atlantic University in Boca Raton. He graduated with a degree in civil engineering from the university in May 2018.

Jeff Driskel Brother | Career

Football Career

An incredibly talented athlete since his teenage years, Jeff Driskel played exceptionally well for the Hagerty High School team until 2014. Apart from recording two rushing touchdowns, he had 13 touchdowns, covering 1,160 yards.

After enrolling at the university for engineering courses, Jeff played limited matches for the team as he redshirted throughout his college career. He recorded three touchdowns and four passing interceptions in nine games in the 2015 season.

Jason started the game for the Owls in his second season. He played 12 games this season and had nine touchdowns and eleven interceptions. 

Despite having a good chance of getting drafted into the NFL, Jason announced that he would quit his football career after his junior season in 2018.

He thanked his family and everyone who supported him during his football journey and said he would focus on academics from then on. In addition, Jason made it clear that he had never thought of having a professional football career ever in his life.

Beyond Football

After graduating college, Jason worked in various positions for different companies, mainly Restaurant Brands International and GoPuff. 

Restaurant Brands International is a fast-food chain with branches across various nations. He spent nearly three years in its Miami branch as an analyst and manager. 

Jason currently works as a strategy and planning manager at GoPuff, a consumer goods and food delivery company. Before that, he worked as a strategy and operation analyst for the same company in its Austin branch.
