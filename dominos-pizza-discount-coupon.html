I haven't paid full price for pizza in over 20 years - my three 'secret discount codes' to score up

ONE fast-food sleuth has shared three discount codes to get you up to 40 percent off your next pizza order.

TikToker user Jordan (@jordan_the_stallion8) recently went viral with his fast food tips.

This time, he posted a TikTok claiming he hasn’t paid full price for a pizza in 20 years.

The self-described “President of the Fast Food Secrets Club” decided to share his discount codes for Domino’s online orders.

Please note these coupon codes may not work in every area.

But The U.S. Sun tested the codes for two addresses in New York City and Los Angeles to verify their authenticity.

Read more fast food news

THE CODES

First, coupon code 9174 can be used to get 40 percent off a large pizza with three toppings ordered for carry-out.

If you prefer a medium pizza, type in code 8682 to get 40 percent off your carry-out order.

But what if you want a discount on any type of order, including delivery?

No problem, coupon code 1126 gives 20 percent off your entire order whether you carry out or get it delivered.

These codes can be entered when ordering online at dominos.com with the option “add coupon” at the bottom of the checkout page.

Domino’s also shares a page of seasonal special coupons and deals online.

MARCH MADNESS

Many pizza fans are looking for a new deal after Domino’s scrapped its 50 percent off all specialty pizzas offer last month.

The discount, made available in conjunction with March Madness, allowed customers to choose from 12 half-price pizzas or make their own.

But the deal was discontinued as of March 26.

MORE FAST FOOD NEWS

Meanwhile, Taco Bell recently made a change to its rewards program by getting rid of a popular redeemable option.

Nachos Bell Grande, which was previously one of the highest-value items available, has been scrapped as a rewards choice.

Fans took to Twitter to share their disappointment at the news.

One person wrote: "Taco Bell took the Nachos Bell Grande off the reward choices? Why would they hurt me like this?”

Diners will now have to shell out $4.99 for the item.
