James Nesbitt sparks rumours his Cold Feet character Adam could become a dad in the new series

JAMES Nesbitt has sparked rumours his Cold Feet character Adam could again become a dad after he was spotted filming scenes in which his on-screen lover pushed a pram.

The Northern Irish star nattered away to ITV actress Leanne Best - his girlfriend Tina in the hit comeback programme - as they walked down a busy street in Manchester.

James, in character as widower Adam, was wrapped up in a chunky black puffa jacket while Leanne has a huge smile on her face as she wheeled the pushchair and stared lovingly at the tot under its hood.

At the end of last series, viewers were left swooning over Adam's love life when Tina agreed to give their relationship a go.

The sweet scenes came about after Adam's son Matt (Cel Spellman) gave his dad the thumbs-up for his new relationship after Adam's wife Rachel was killed off in series 5.

It seems some very good news - in the form of a new baby - could be on the cards when the show returns to screens.

Later in the day, things weren't looking so positive for Adam.

After spending what appeared to be some quality family time with Tina, he switched to a snazzy grey suit jacket and matching trousers as he ran errands.

Yet his new outfit was all in vain when he was soaked through after a passing car drove through a huge puddle.

The grimace on his very tanned face was clear as the spray of water hit him straight in the eye.

The true extent of his dousing was revealed just minutes later when he tried to hail a car - with his blazer sopping wet and dark hair plastered to his head.

His hair is bound to again be a topic of conversation for show fans after producers weaved aspects of James' real-life hair transplant into the plotline.

Viewers were equally in hysterics over his "painted on" eyebrows.

Last year, Cold Feet made its triumphant return to ITV after a 13-year absence.

From the word go it was like Adam, David, Karen, Pete and Jenny – played by James, Robert Bathurst, Hermione Norris, John Thomson and Fay Ripley – had never been away.

Yet Fay confessed she thought it was a "practical joke" when she was asked to make her small screen comeback.
