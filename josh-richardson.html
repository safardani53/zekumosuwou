Josh Richardson: Early Life, Family & Education

On September 15, 1993, Richardson was born to Micheal and Alice Richardson in Edmond, Oklahoma, United States. He has an older sister named Alex Richardson.

Richardson’s father was a firefighter. His mother was a lieutenant colonel in US Air Force Reserve.

Both his parents were Christians, and Richardson instilled Christian values in him. So he followed Christianity as his parents’ religion influenced him from a very young age. 

As his mother was in the military, his upbringing was influenced by it. The discipline and hard work for the craft are installed in him from his background and military parent upbringing.

His mother taught him to be focused and disciplined in life and gave him lessons about essential values and ethics in achieving his goals in life. 

Richardson was always interested in sports from a young age. He played football in his early childhood. His older sister used to play basketball, and eventually, Richardson was attracted to the game by watching his sister play. In the end, he focused on basketball as his main interest with complete dedication.

Seeing his interest in basketball, his parents agreed to let him choose his passion, which motivated him to play and take the sport more seriously.

His father used to teach him basketball skills, and his mother always told him to go to the gym so that he would be physically fit to play the game and maintain a healthy physique.

Education

With years of training from childhood to teenage life, Richardson went to high school, Santa Fe High School, and started playing basketball professionally.

After graduating from high school, Josh went to the University of Tennessee for his college studies, where he continued his basketball career. He has a degree in psychology.

Josh Richardson: Career

High School and College Career

In his senior year at high school, Josh was selected in the Large West All-State Team and The Oklahoman’s Super 5 first team by the Oklahoma Coaches Association.

Richardson went to the University of Tennessee after graduating from high school and collecting the scores of a rising basketball player. He played college basketball.

There was a vast improvement in his game skills and performance during his college years. The experience helped him sharpen his skills and techniques.

With such hard work and dedication, Richardson was selected to the First Team All-SEC, the SEC All-Defensive Team, and the Defensive All-American Team by the official.

NBA Debut

Richardson made his NBA debut on November 5, against the Minnesota Timberwolves after the first four seasons. 

In December 2015, Richardson was exchanged to the Sioux Falls Skyforce, but he was back with the Miami Heat the following month. 

Richardson played very well in his rookie year with the Miami Heat. He showcased his excellent game and earned the Eastern Conference Rookie of the Month in March 2016. He was the third player from the team to win the title.

Contract Extension with Miami Heat

On September 18, 2017, Richardson extended his contract with Miami Heat for another four years with $42 million. During his contract with the Heat, he played well and improved. 

Richardson improved his basketball skills and performance and scored many points. He was with the team till 2019. At the end of the season, Richardson suffered from heel and groin injuries and had to miss out on the remaining games.

Despite the injury, Richardson averaged 16.6 points and 4.1 assists in his matches which were the highest of his NBA career.

On July 6, 2019, Richardson was exchanged to the Philadelphia 76ers by Miami Heat. The coach of the 76ers praised his skills and performance during his playing career and named him the dot connecter and bridge-builder on the team. He played for the 76ers for a year.

On November 18, 2020, Richardson was exchanged to the Dallas Mavericks by the 76ers. He could not play well during his stay with the team. And his performance was called disappointing by the officials.

Traded Off to San Antonio Spurs

On July 31, 2021, Richardson was exchanged to the Boston Celtics. He played for a year with the team and then was traded to San Antonio Spurs on February 10, 2022. Derrick White was exchanged to Boston Celtics by the Spurs for Richardson. 

The Spurs picked Richardson in mid-season with colossal risk, but the gamble has been paid off as he can deliver all the expectations and perform well.

Until April 2022, Richardson has played 19 games for the Spurs and has scored excellent points proving his high IQ in basketball.

San Antonio Spurs picked Richardson with an annual salary of $11,615,328, and he has proven his worth as a fantastic player for the team.

Knee Injury

Richardson joined the Miami Heat for the NBA Summer League 2016. He played well on the team and scored many points.

On September 9, 2016, Richardson suffered from a knee injury. His right knee was injured, and the medicals reported that there had been a collateral ligament injury, and because of the injury, he missed out on eight weeks of the game.

Richardson was back in the game on December 27, 2016, and scored his career-high points in the match against the Oklahoma City Thunder, but his team lost the game.
