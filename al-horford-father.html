Al Horford Father: All About Tito Horford! [2023 Update]

Tito Horford, originally Alfredo William “Tito” Horford, is a Dominican retired professional basketball player and the father of five-time NBA All-Star basketball player Al Horford aka “Big Al.”

Tito played center his whole career for two teams in the National Basketball Association (NBA); Milwaukee Bucks from 1988 to 1990 and Washington Bullets (now Washington Wizards) from 1993 to 1994.

Aside from that, the first Dominican-born player to play in the NBA played in Spain in 1992.

The University of Miami alumnus was listed on the Bucks’ summer 1992 roster.

His loving son Al Horford currently plays center and power forward for the Boston Celtics of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

The 37– year-old Horford had to stay out of the game against Orlando Magic because of low back stiffness on October 22, 2022, even though he planned to play back-to-back this season.

Moreover, son Al has played for the Dominican Republic national basketball team since 2008.

Short Bio On Al Horford

Al Horford, aka “Big Al,” is the son of the former NBA player Tito Horford and Arelis Reynoso.

The center for the Boston Celtics of the National Basketball Association, Al is the highest-paid Latin American basketball player and a five-time NBA All-Star.

Born in Puerto Plata, Dominican Republic, Al went to Grand Ledge High School in Grand Ledge, Michigan, United States.

After high school graduation, he accepted an athletic scholarship to attend the University of Florida and played college basketball for Florida Gators’ men’s basketball team.

Al was drafted third overall by the Atlanta Hawks in the 2007 NBA Draft.

He played with the Hawks for nine seasons before signing with the Boston Celtics as a free agent in 2016.

He stayed with the Celtics for three seasons and signed with the Philadelphia 76ers in the 2019 oof-season.

Al was then traded to Thunder in the 2020 off-season. And ultimately, before the 2021 season, Al was again traded to the Celtics, with whom he reached the NBA finals.

Aside from NBA, Al has played for the Dominican national basketball team since 2008. In fact, he won a bronze medal at the FIBA Americas Championship in 2011.
