Will Ospreay | Career

Will, currently regarded as one of the best professional wrestlers in the world, started his career in the UK in 2012 on the independent circuit.

He started working with the Japanese company New Japan Pro Wrestling in 2016 as a member of the junior heavyweight class after being referred by AJ Styles.

Because AJ Styles was Will’s childhood idol, it makes it all the more meaningful that he was able to sign with New Japan Pro Wrestling in the way he did.

Styles was the role model and inspiration for Will Ospreay, and the fact that he assisted him in traveling to New Japan brings them together.

Further, Ospreay’s striking prowess and his ability to seamlessly incorporate high-flying components into his technique indeed resemble those of AJ in some ways.

Will won the 2016 and 2019 Best of the Super Juniors and became a three-time IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion. In 2019, Ospreay also won the NEVER Openweight Championship.

That same year, the wrestler participated in the most fantastic NJPW competition for heavyweight wrestlers, the G1 Climax. He switched to the heavyweight class in 2020, where he went on to win the IWGP World Heavyweight Championship.

Frontline Wrestling 

Ospreay declared in 2018 that he would launch Frontline Wrestling as his promotion. The wrestler said Frontline would be a British Puroresu-style organization that aspires to restore wrestling’s status as a sport in England.

They held their inaugural event, “Build Me An Empire,” on June 28, 2018. When wrestlers decide to promote themselves, it usually happens after retirement when they seek new opportunities to give back to the industry.

Ospreay’s advancement was just temporary. Will, however, sold Frontline on the promotion’s first anniversary. He did so to relocate to Japan and pursue a full-time schedule with New Japan Pro Wrestling.

However, his involvement in the activity in the first place demonstrates his interest in the industry.

Beretta and FTR defeated Davis and Fletcher of the Aussie Open and Will of the United Empire on June 10, 2022’s AEW Rampage.

After the match, Will and United Empire stablemates attacked FTR and Roppongi Vice again, interrupted by Orange Cassidy, who stared down Ospreay.

Due to this high-risk approach, Will’s fans and well-wishers are concerned about his health and career longevity. This included a neck injury he sustained on April 1, 2018, when competing against Marty Scurll.
