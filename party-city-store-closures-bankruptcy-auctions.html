Major retailer with hundreds of locations to close 22 stores this month after bankruptcy- see the fu

PARTY City will be closing over 20 stores before the month is through after filing for bankruptcy.

The beloved party supplier's next step is to close the doors of several locations after disproportionately low sales.

Festive gathering retail giant Party City has put 12 stores located across six different states up for auction.

Another ten stores are expected to close by the end of the month - with the possibility of more closures in the future.

It all comes after the company filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in January, as reported by MSN.

The retailer is now asking to get out of leases for 28 vacant stores in 13 states, which closed before the bankruptcy filing, per Retail Dive.

The business operated 823 stores and 770 were company-owned as of January, court documents showed.

Supply chain issues led to merchandise hiccups and helium has been in short supply, all factors contributing to the closures, according to Reuters.

PARTY'S OVER

The first Party City store opened 1986 in New Jersey.

Party City saw a slow demise over Covid-19, when the market for get-togethers was a scarce one.

The move to file for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection allows a business to keep trading while it restructures its debts.

Franchise stores, subsidiaries outside of the U.S., and Party City's foil balloons business are not part of the restructuring.

The company had more than 16,000 full- and part-time employees in 2021.

High costs of products and labor paired with lower sales have put the retailer in a tough spot.

It also faces competition from Walmart and Target, as well as occasion-based pop-up stores like Spirt Halloween.

Party City executives also revealed its Halloween sales weren't as high as they expected.
